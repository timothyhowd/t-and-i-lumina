How Lumina works

Lumina drafts employment documents through a conversation. A specialist describes the situation in plain language; Lumina identifies the right document, gathers the necessary context, surfaces relevant constraints, and produces a draft. The specialist reviews, revises, and signs off.

Behind the conversation, three engines work together. None of them is exposed in the user experience — but each contributes to the final document.

Templates

For standard, high-volume situations, Lumina uses approved templates with structural correctness enforced — variables, signature flow, retention category. Adding a new template is configuration, not engineering.

Precedent corpus

The historical document library is indexed and queryable. When a request doesn't fit a clean template, Lumina retrieves comparable past documents and synthesizes from precedent — with the specialist confirming each output.

Live regulation

For jurisdictions with machine-readable regulatory feeds, Lumina queries authoritative sources at draft time. Every clause traces back to a specific statute, collective bargaining agreement, or regulatory provision — visible as footnotes on the document.

Where humans remain in the loop

The specialist is always in the conversation — Lumina cannot finalize a document on its own. Beyond that, three classes of cases route to additional human review:

  • First-time generations for any new (event × country × worker class) combination
  • High-stakes outputs — senior compensation, cross-border employment, performance terminations
  • Regulation changes — first ten outputs after a rule update receive mandatory legal review

What's logged

Every conversation, every piece of context surfaced by the agent, every choice the specialist made, and every regulatory citation on the final document — together they form the audit record. The conversation itself is part of the legal evidence, not just the document.

Coverage today

Lumina supports document generation across employment lifecycle events: hires, role changes, compensation adjustments, amendments, terminations, and certificates. Active jurisdictions: Finland, Spain, United States, Germany, United Kingdom. Active brand entities: DoorDash, Wolt, Deliveroo.
